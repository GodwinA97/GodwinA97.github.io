City of Leeds

What is Leeds?

Leeds is a city in West Yorkshire, England. It is the largest settlement in Yorkshire and the administrative centre of the City of Leeds Metropolitan Borough, which is the second most populous district in the United Kingdom.

It is built around the River Aire and is in the eastern foothills of the Pennines. The name Leeds is derived from the Celtic word Ladenses, which means “people of the fast-flowing river”, in reference to the River Aire that flows through the city.

Leeds’s core city has a population of about 500,000, making it the eighth-largest city in England after London, Birmingham, Liverpool, Nottingham, Sheffield, Bristol, and Leicester.

Leeds is also doing great in the educational field as it is referred to as a Best University Destination in the UK. The University of Leeds gets more applications than any other famous University in the country. The two top-rated and internationally recognised universities of Leeds hold more than 75,000 students who are pursuing their degree education there. More than 10% of total UK graduate students were also educated in this city.

92% of the population of Leeds speak the Northern accent of English, also called the Yorkshire dialect. The rest speak Polish, Urdu, Punjabi, etc.

Leeds’ history dates back to the 5th century when it was part of the Kingdom of Elmet. The name “Leeds” is derived from “Loidis,” an old Celtic word. The city’s growth surged during the Industrial Revolution, transforming it into a significant hub for wool, flax, and iron industries. Today, Leeds is known for its vibrant economy and diverse cultural offerings, making it a key player in the region.
